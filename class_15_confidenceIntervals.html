Standardised sampling distribution of mean

  • Standardised cut-off values around zero (same abs. value)

Standardised sampling distribution of mean

  • Easily applicable to any population parameters…
  • \(\bar{X}_{2.5\%} = \mu \pm Z_{2.5\%} * \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{N}}\)
  • … and other cut-off points (e.g., \(Z_{0.5\%}\))

Confidence intervals

Confidence interval for population mean

\(CI_{p\%} = \bar{X} \pm Z_{p\%} * \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{N}}\)

Interval estimation

  • p% confidence interval: We have p % confidence that the sample comes from a population with the parameter falling within the interval.

  • \(CI_{p\%} = \bar{X} \pm Z_{p\%} * \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{N}}\)

    • \(\bar{X}\): sample mean
    • \(N\): sample size
    • \(\sigma\): population standard deviation
    • \(Z_{p\%}\) value related to confidence level p (qnorm(p/2))

Exercises on CI:

  • 30 CogSci students were sampled at random and their mean IQ is 117. Assuming that standard deviation in the population of CogSci students is the same as in general population, construct 99% CI around the mean. How does it relate to the general population mean IQ? What does it tell us?

  • 20 Psychology students were sampled at random and their mean IQ is 105. Assuming that standard deviation in the population of Psychology students is the same as in general population, can we say with 95% confidence level that mean IQ of Psychology students differs from the general population mean IQ?

Solutions (CI):

> #Ex.1
> 117 + qnorm(.995) * 15 / sqrt(30)
> 117 - qnorm(.995) * 15 / sqrt(30)
> # General population mean outside CI
> # CogSci population different
> 
> #Ex.2
> 105 + qnorm(.975) * 15 / sqrt(20)
> 105 - qnorm(.975) * 15 / sqrt(20)
> # General population mean within CI
> # Psych population not different??

t distribution

  • Modify the simulation accordingly
    • using qt()
> i <- 10000
> mu <- 165; sigma <- 5; N <- 20
> mean(replicate(i, {
+    s <- rnorm(N, mu, sigma)

+ }))

t distribution and CIs – exercise

  • \(CI_{p\%} = \bar{X} \pm t_{p\%}(df) * \frac{s}{\sqrt{N}}\)

  • 25 people were randomly sampled, their mean shoe size is 40 and the standard deviation of their shoe sizes is 3.

  • Using t distribution, calculate the upper limit of 95% CI around mean.

> 40 + qt(.975, 24) * 3 / sqrt(25)
